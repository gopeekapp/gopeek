What is GoPeek?

Your browser shouldn't explode into 47 tabs just to check a few links.

GoPeek replaces the exhausting "open in new tab → wait → check → close tab" workflow with live interactive windows that open instantly and directly inside your current page.

Instead of constantly losing context, you can browse links, compare active pages side-by-side, minimize Peeks into floating bubbles, and continue navigating without interruption.

Most links are just curiosity clicks. GoPeek lets you satisfy them instantly without destroying your flow.

FAQ

Questions? Answered.

Everything you need to know about GoPeek before you install.

How does GoPeek work?
Hold Shift (customizable to Alt/Ctrl) and hover over any link. GoPeek opens a live, fully interactive window directly on your current page. You can browse it naturally. No new tabs created, no context lost.
Is GoPeek free?
Yes. GoPeek is completely free to use. All core features, including hover preview, sidebar mode, multi-peek, bubble minimize, and dynamic theme matching, are available at no cost. We may introduce a Pro tier with advanced features in the future, but the essentials will always stay free.
Which browsers does GoPeek support?
GoPeek is available on Firefox and Microsoft Edge right now. A Chrome version is in development and coming soon.
Does GoPeek track my browsing data?
No. GoPeek does not collect, store, or transmit any browsing data, history, or personal information. The extension operates entirely locally in your browser. We use privacy-friendly analytics on our website, but the extension itself has zero tracking. Read our full Privacy Policy for details.
Can I customize the keyboard shortcut?
Yes. The default trigger is Shift + hover, but you can change it to Alt, or Ctrl in the GoPeek popup settings. You can also adjust window size, by either dragging GoPeek window's bottom-right corner or manually setting it to desired resolution in the popup. You can also adjust the sidebar width to match your workflow.
Does GoPeek work on all websites?
GoPeek works on most websites, including Google Search, Reddit, Wikipedia, GitHub, Stack Overflow, news sites, and blogs. Some sites with strict Content Security Policy (CSP) headers may block iframe previews, but GoPeek automatically handles most of these cases. If a specific site does not work, let us know and we will investigate.
