GoPeek vs OneTab: Which is Better for Your Browser?

June 16, 2026 · 6 min read · By GoPeek Team

OneTab cleans up the mess. GoPeek stops you from making it.

OneTab and GoPeek solve the same problem — too many tabs — but they approach it from opposite directions. OneTab cleans up tabs after you open them. GoPeek prevents tabs from being opened in the first place.

Both are useful. Both are free. But they fit different workflows. Here is the direct comparison.

What OneTab Does

OneTab is a cleanup tool. You click its icon and all your open tabs collapse into a single list page. The tabs are closed. Their processes are killed. Your RAM drops by 80-90% instantly. The list is saved. You can restore individual tabs or the whole session later. You can also export the list as a shareable page.

OneTab is great for emergencies. Your browser is crawling. You have 40 tabs open. You need RAM now. Click the OneTab icon. Everything collapses. Your machine breathes again.

OneTab is also good for session saving. You are researching a topic. You have 15 relevant tabs open. You want to save the set for later. OneTab exports them as a single URL you can bookmark or share.

But OneTab does not prevent tabs. It only cleans them up. Tomorrow you will open 30 more tabs. Next Friday you will collapse them again. The cycle repeats. OneTab is a broom, not a fence.

What GoPeek Does

GoPeek is a prevention tool. It lets you preview links without opening tabs. Hold Shift, hover over a link, and a live preview of the page opens in a floating window. You read what you need. You scroll. You click inside it. When you are done, you close it. No tab. No process. No RAM.

GoPeek also has sidebar mode for references you need while working, multi-peek for comparing multiple sources side-by-side, and bubble minimize for pages you want to keep accessible without adding to your tab bar.

GoPeek stops the problem at the source. Most tabs are opened for glances, not work. A preview handles the glance. A tab is only opened when the page becomes your primary workspace. Your daily tab creation drops by 70-80%.

Three Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Friday Emergency

OneTab: You have 35 tabs open. Chrome is at 6GB RAM. Your fan is loud. You click the OneTab icon. All 35 tabs collapse into a list. RAM drops to 800MB. You feel relief. You bookmark the list. Next Monday you restore 5 tabs and ignore the rest.

GoPeek: Not helpful here. GoPeek does not collapse existing tabs. If you already have 35 tabs, you need a cleanup tool first.

Winner: OneTab. Emergency cleanup is its specialty.

Scenario 2: The Research Session

OneTab: You are reading an article with 8 source links. You open all 8 in new tabs. You now have 9 tabs. You read each one. Some are useful. Some are garbage. You have 9 tabs open for the next 3 hours. At the end, you collapse them with OneTab. You created 8 tabs you did not need.

GoPeek: You preview each of the 8 links. You close 6 immediately. You sidebar the 2 useful ones while you write. Total tabs created: 1 (the original article). Total RAM used: minimal. No cleanup needed.

Winner: GoPeek. Prevention beats cleanup.

Scenario 3: The Project Archive

OneTab: You finish a research project with 12 relevant tabs. You want to save the set for a report next month. OneTab collapses them into a shareable list. You bookmark the list. Next month you restore all 12 tabs with one click.

GoPeek: Not helpful here. GoPeek does not save sessions. You would need to bookmark each page individually or use a different tool.

Winner: OneTab. Session saving is its strength.

When to Use OneTab

Use OneTab if:

  • You already have 20+ tabs open and need RAM now
  • You want to save a set of tabs as a shareable collection
  • You do research projects and need to archive sessions
  • You are not ready to change your browsing habits yet
  • You want a one-click emergency button for tab overload

When to Use GoPeek

Use GoPeek if:

  • You open tabs for glances and close them within 5 minutes
  • You want to check sources without losing your current page
  • You compare multiple pages side-by-side
  • You reference documentation while coding or writing
  • You are tired of the Friday tab purge

Why Prevention Beats Cleanup

OneTab is reactive. GoPeek is proactive. Both have value, but prevention scales better.

If you use OneTab every day, you are treating a symptom. You open too many tabs, then you clean them up. The next day you do it again. It is a cycle.

If you use GoPeek, you break the cycle. You stop opening tabs for glances. Your tab count stays low without daily cleanup. Your RAM stays low without emergency collapses. Your focus stays intact without context switches.

That does not mean OneTab is useless. It means OneTab is a backup plan, not a primary strategy. Use GoPeek for daily browsing. Keep OneTab installed for emergencies and session saving.

The stack: GoPeek for daily prevention. OneTab for emergency cleanup and session archives. They work together. But if you only install one, choose based on whether you need to fix the problem (GoPeek) or manage the mess (OneTab).

The Bottom Line

OneTab and GoPeek are not competitors. They are tools for different stages of the same problem. OneTab handles the aftermath. GoPeek handles the cause.

If your browser is already full, OneTab is the faster fix. If you want to stop your browser from getting full, GoPeek is the better fix. Most users benefit from both.

Install GoPeek for daily use. Install OneTab for emergencies. Your tab bar will stay clean. Your RAM will stay low. And your Fridays will be purge-free.
