Want Arc’s "Peek" Feature but Love Chrome? Here’s Your Fix

June 14, 2026 · 5 min read · By GoPeek Team

Arc Peek is great. Being locked to Arc is not. GoPeek works on the browser you already use.

Arc Browser has a feature called Peek. You hover a link, press a key, and a preview window opens. You can read the page, scroll it, and close it without ever leaving your current tab. It is fast. It is clean. It is the best native link preview in any browser.

There is one problem. Arc is Mac-only. And even on Mac, Arc is a full browser replacement. It replaces your tabs with a sidebar. It replaces your bookmarks with a command palette. It replaces your address bar with a floating search box. Some people love this. Others — especially Chrome users with years of extensions, passwords, and workflows — do not want to rebuild their entire browser just to get link previews.

You should not have to switch browsers to get a good preview feature. And you do not. GoPeek brings the same instant, interactive link preview to Chrome, Edge, and Firefox. Hold Shift, hover any link, and browse the destination in a live window. No tab created. No context switch. No browser migration required.

What Arc Peek Actually Does

Arc Peek is simple. You see a link. You press Cmd + Shift (or hover with a modifier). A small preview window slides in from the side. It loads the page. You can scroll it, click internal links, and read the content. You press Escape or click away and it vanishes. Your original tab never lost focus.

It is designed for exactly one thing: checking a link without committing to it. Reading a source article without opening a tab. Verifying a GitHub repo without adding it to your tab bar. Glancing at a news headline without polluting your workspace.

The problem is not the feature. The problem is the container. Arc Peek only works inside Arc. And Arc is a walled garden.

Why People Won’t Switch to Arc

Arc has fans. It also has dealbreakers. Here is why Chrome users stay put.

  • Platform lock. Arc is macOS only. If you use Windows, Linux, or ChromeOS, you cannot use Arc at all. Peek is irrelevant to half the market.
  • Extension compatibility. Arc’s sidebar-first design breaks some extensions. Password managers, developer tools, and certain ad blockers behave differently or not at all.
  • Workflow disruption. Chrome users have years of synced passwords, bookmarks, history, and autofill. Moving to Arc means rebuilding all of it.
  • Vertical tabs are mandatory. Arc does not have a horizontal tab bar. If you prefer traditional tabs, Arc forces you to adapt.
  • Closed ecosystem. Arc is not open source. It is venture-backed. It could change direction, add paywalls, or shut down. Chrome and Firefox are not going anywhere.

Peek is not worth switching browsers for. It is worth adding to your current browser. That is what GoPeek does.

The Arc trap: Peek is good enough to make you want Arc. But Arc is different enough to make you regret it. Do not switch browsers for one feature. Install one extension instead.

What GoPeek Does That Arc Peek Cannot

GoPeek is not a copy of Arc Peek. It is a superset. Arc Peek previews one link in a fixed panel. GoPeek previews links with multiple modes designed for different workflows.

Hover Preview

Hold Shift, hover a link, and a preview window opens instantly. This is the direct equivalent to Arc Peek. It works on any link, any site, any page. Close it with Escape or by moving your mouse away. Your original page never loses focus.

Sidebar Mode

Drag the preview to the edge of your screen and it snaps into a split-screen sidebar. This is better than Arc’s fixed split view because you control the width. You can make it narrow for a quick reference check or wide for side-by-side reading. Your main page stays on the left. The preview stays on the right. You can scroll both independently.

Multi-Peek

Arc Peek shows one preview at a time. GoPeek can show multiple. Open 3 previews side by side to compare products, sources, or documentation pages. This is impossible in Arc without opening actual tabs.

Bubble Minimize

Double-click the preview header and it collapses into a floating bubble — a small circle with the site’s favicon that sits on your screen. It is not a tab. It is not in your tab bar. It is a visual reminder that you have something to revisit. Click it to expand. Close it when done. Arc has nothing like this.

How to Use GoPeek on Chrome

You do not need to learn a new browser. You install an extension and change one habit.

  • Step 1: Install GoPeek from the Chrome Web Store.
  • Step 2: Hold Shift and hover any link on any page.
  • Step 3: The preview opens. Scroll it. Click inside it. Navigate it.
  • Step 4: Press Escape or move your mouse away to close it.
  • Step 5: For side-by-side work, drag the preview to the edge of your screen. It snaps into sidebar mode.

That is it. No new browser. No new shortcuts to memorize. No migration of passwords or bookmarks. You stay in Chrome. You get the preview feature. And you get more modes than Arc offers.
